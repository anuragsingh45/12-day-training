Segment 3: The Channel Partner (CP) Profile

Who is channel partner? A channel partner is a builder's official marketing partner. As the builder announces the new launch he call all channel partners for meeting and tell them the configuration and the pricing he is going to launch at his project. he said, to channel partner to start marketing and sell their product. On selling, the channel partner will get some percentage of brokerage from the developer.

Normally in real estate, there are three phases of property:

  • Pre-launch phase
  • Under Construction phase
  • Ready to Move or Sustenance Phase

Mostly channel partner works in the pre-launch and under-construction phases.

Profileing of channel partner:

  • Individual channel partner.
  • Small firm channel partners they have team of 4-5 people.
  • Big firm channel partners they have more than 10 employees and they work on two types of projects first one is AOP (Annual Operation Plan) and other is the Retail segment.

The POC: The owner is your main Point of Contact. They are sophisticated and business-savvy. Approach them as a consultant, not a salesperson.

Day 6: Mock Sessions

Activity: Conduct mock F2F meetings with trainers and senior RMs playing the role of the client. Receive constructive feedback on your presentation, communication, and objection handling skills.

Day 7, 8, & 9: On-Field Meetings & Objection Handling

Activity: Accompany senior RMs on actual client meetings. Observe how they build rapport, present solutions, and handle real-world objections. Participate in post-meeting debriefs.

Day 10: Your First Meeting (Shadowed)

Activity: You will lead your own client meeting from start to finish. A senior RM or Team Lead will accompany you to observe and provide support if needed.

Day 11: Independent Meetings

Activity: You will conduct your own client meetings independently. The focus is on applying all the training from the previous days in a live environment.

Day 12: Review Meeting

Objective: A comprehensive review of your performance with your Team Lead and the Sales Head.

Agenda:

  • Discuss feedback from mock calls and on-field meetings.
  • Review your understanding of products and processes.
  • Address any remaining questions or challenges.
  • Set clear performance goals for your first 30 days as a qualified Relationship Manager.
